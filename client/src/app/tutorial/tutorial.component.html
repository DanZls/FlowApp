About

FlowApp implements a mathematical algorithm of hydrodynamics for calculating fluid dynamics around solid boundaries.

The model represents view from above on a rectangular area of a hypothetical unbounded in any direction pool, filled with fluid (e.g. ocean), which is considered as ideal fluid. Static boundaries are borders of immovable solid objects (e.g. islands). Dynamic boundaries are borders of a shape, formed by liquid, lighter than the main one and floating on surface of the pool (e.g. an oil spill on water); this shape can move freely with the underlying masses of the main liquid, moved by current.

Tutorial

Click on button "Add model".

In the opened window, click on the board to add points to the static boundary. Then click "Next" and add points to the dynamic boundary. Currently, FlowApp allows to add only one static and one dynamic borders to each model.

The points should be added in clockwise order around the boundary. Both boundaries should contain at least 3 points each and be closed. You should not worry about closing a boundary though, since it is closed automatically when 3 points are added.

Avoid building such models, where a dynamic boundary flows around a static one on both sides. This case is currently one of the weak spots of the calculating algorithm where it becomes unstable.

Before you submit the model for calculation, fill the parameters. You can also leave the default ones, which are balanced for optimal accuracy and performance.

The parameters are: Flow Velocity - the velocity of current in vector form (default is from left to right); Simulation Time and Simulation Time Step - time parameters, the product of their values gives the number of animation frames generated (default gives 500 frames); Min Points Spacing and Max Points Spacing - minimal and maximum distances between points of boundaries (less is more accurate, but cuts into performance).

Click on "Calculate and Save" button. The calculation will take some time (timeout 30s). After this, the model list will reload with the calculated model included. If the calculation times out, the new model will included less frames than it should.

If any problems or failures occure during the calculation or other server-side operations, they will be reflected in "Logs" along with success messages.
